Custom CNC Bottle Opener

Project Overview

Custom-designed bottle opener showcasing precision CNC woodworking with decorative inlay techniques.

Design Concept

Created functional bottle openers using cherry wood as the primary material with walnut inlay accents. The design combines practical functionality with aesthetic appeal, demonstrating the capabilities of CNC machining for small-scale product manufacturing.

Fabrication Process

Material Selection: Cherry wood for main body provides excellent workability and attractive grain patterns. Walnut inlay creates visual contrast and adds durability to high-wear areas.

CNC Machining: Precise toolpath planning for clean edges and smooth surfaces. Careful attention to feeds and speeds for hardwood machining to prevent tearout and ensure quality surface finish.

Inlay Technique: Routed pockets for walnut inlay pieces, ensuring tight fit and flush final surface. Tested multiple glue-up methods for secure, long-lasting assembly.

Finishing

Explored various finishing techniques including food-safe oils and lacquers. Learned the impact of different finish types on wood grain enhancement and durability for handled objects.

Technical Challenges

  • Optimizing toolpaths for hardwood without burning or tearout
  • Achieving precise inlay pocket depths for flush final surface
  • Selecting appropriate finish for durability and food safety
  • Managing grain direction for both aesthetics and structural integrity

Results

Produced functional, attractive bottle openers that demonstrate precision CNC craftsmanship. Developed reliable workflow for small-batch custom product fabrication.

What I Learned

Gained hands-on experience with hardwood CNC parameters, inlay techniques, and finishing methods. Understanding how material properties affect tooling decisions and final product quality. Importance of proper grain orientation in both functional and aesthetic considerations.

Soft Robotic End-Effector ‚Äì International Collaboration

Project Overview

Timeline: April 2024 ‚Äì May 2025 (Capstone Project)
Team: International Collaboration | George Mason University
Award: Fall 2024 ‚Äì Spring 2025

Design Goal

Collaborated with an international team to design a soft gripper for gentle handling in medical and agricultural applications. The goal was to create a pneumatically-actuated soft robotic gripper capable of safely manipulating delicate objects without damage.

Development Process

Pneumatic System: Developed and tested a pneumatic actuation soft gripper using 3D-printed molds and silicone casting, achieving proper air channels and flexible actuation for controlled gripping force.

Electronics Integration: Designed and integrated a compact electronics case onto a mobile robotic platform using Chainext VEX for control, improving portability, protection, and wire-routing capability for the complete system.

Technical Challenges

  • Creating air-tight pneumatic channels in silicone using 3D-printed molds
  • Achieving consistent actuation and proper gripping force control
  • Integrating electronics compactly while maintaining accessibility
  • Iterating on mold designs for optimal silicone casting results
  • Coordinating with international team members across time zones

Results

Successfully created a functional soft robotic gripper with reliable pneumatic actuation. The design demonstrated the ability to handle delicate objects safely, and the modular electronics integration made the system practical for real-world testing scenarios.

What I Learned

Advanced manufacturing techniques in silicone casting, pneumatic system design, international team collaboration, and the importance of iterative prototyping in achieving reliable soft robotics performance.

Rosie Wildlife Tracker

üèÜ Award

3rd Place - GMU Hackfax 2025 "A Better World" Theme

Project Overview

We built a compact, non-invasive wildlife tracking device using an ESP32 microcontroller, paired with a GY-521 accelerometer/gyroscope, pulse sensor, and GPS module. The system monitors an animal's movement, location, and heart rate, providing insights into its behavior, stress, and health in real time.

Goal

Develop a non-invasive wildlife tracking device that can be safely attached to animals to monitor their location, movement, stress, and overall health in real time. The data collected is transmitted wirelessly using Wi-Fi or LoRa to a cloud-based database hosted on Oracle Cloud, where it can be analyzed by researchers through an intuitive web dashboard or processed using AI models to identify behavioral patterns and health trends.

Technical Implementation

Hardware Components:

  • ESP32 microcontroller for data collection and wireless transmission
  • GY-521 accelerometer/gyroscope for movement tracking
  • Pulse sensor for heart rate and stress monitoring
  • GPS module for real-time location tracking
  • Compact design fitting in 100mm x 70mm enclosure

Power & Deployment: Lightweight and power-efficient with deep-sleep modes to extend field deployment. Designed for non-invasive attachment to small to medium-sized animals.

Data Transmission: Wi-Fi or LoRa protocols for wireless communication to cloud platform. Real-time data streaming enables immediate monitoring of animal health and behavior.

Accomplishments

Successfully demonstrated a working prototype that combines:

  • Real-time GPS tracking for movement patterns
  • Heartbeat monitoring for stress detection
  • Motion sensing using accelerometer and gyroscope
  • 3D-printed, animal-mountable case
  • Centralized system for wireless data transmission and visualization via Oracle Cloud

Future Development

WordPress Dashboard: During the hackathon, we began building a WordPress-based website hosted on Oracle Cloud to display real-time and historical data. Backend infrastructure was successfully deployed, with full integration of live sensor data still in progress.

AI-Powered Analytics: Planning to implement machine learning to recognize behavioral patterns such as frequently visited locations, daily activity cycles, and changes in stress levels over time, providing conservationists with deeper automated insights.

System Expansion: Future enhancements include solar charging, long-range communication improvements, and AI-based behavior recognition to create a truly intelligent tool for wildlife monitoring and ecological research.

Impact

This recognition validates our mission to build smarter, low-cost tools for wildlife conservation. We're excited to continue developing Rosie into a deployable field-ready system that equips conservationists, scientists, and wildlife organizations with better decision-making tools for a more sustainable and biodiverse world.
